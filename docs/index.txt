LOOM
Privacy Policy
Effective Date: April 15, 2026
Loom is a personal planning, fulfillment, goal, reflection, and AI-assisted productivity app. This Privacy Policy explains what information Loom collects, how it is collected, how it is used, when it is shared, and what choices you have.
1. What Loom Collects
Account and contact data
	•	Name and email address
	•	Authentication provider and account identifiers used by Firebase Authentication, Sign in with Apple, Google Sign-In, or email/password sign-in
	•	Subscription entitlement status and current plan label used by the app
Content you create in Loom
	•	Purpose, vision, passions, fulfillment areas, goals, plans, action blocks, little wins, reflections, notes, and related archives or progress records
	•	Diagnostic and onboarding answers, personalization snapshots, personality/profile outputs, and other product-generated insights
	•	LoomAI chat messages, prompts, and related context used to generate replies or suggestions
	•	Feedback submissions, support details, ratings, and message text you choose to send through Loom
Shared, imported, and attached content
	•	Text, links, images, videos, and files shared into Loom from other apps through the Share into Loom extension
	•	Photos or files you import into actions or plans
	•	Metadata and previews for links when Loom renders link previews
Optional permission-based data
	•	Apple Health data you choose to connect, such as steps, workout minutes, sleep, or selected outcome metrics
		•	Camera images, imported photo attachments, and Photos library saves when you use Loom camera or attachment features
	•	Reminder/task data from Apple Reminders, Google Tasks, or Microsoft To Do if you connect those integrations
Usage, diagnostics, and app state
	•	App interaction events, product usage, and feature telemetry collected by Firebase Analytics in production builds
	•	Crash and performance diagnostics collected by Firebase Crashlytics in production builds
	•	Local app settings, session flags, cached previews, notification settings, and other saved preferences
Billing and Apple-managed purchase data
	•	StoreKit product and entitlement status used to unlock features
	•	Renewal status and subscription period information returned by Apple
	•	Loom does not receive your full Apple payment card details
2. How Loom Collects Information
	•	Directly from you when you create an account, sign in, complete onboarding, type content, chat with LoomAI, submit feedback, or configure plans and goals
	•	From your device and app use through local storage, notifications settings, analytics, diagnostics, and saved app state
		•	From Apple frameworks and system services you choose to use, such as CloudKit, HealthKit, EventKit reminders, camera, Photos, and notifications
	•	From connected third-party account integrations you choose to enable, such as Google Sign-In, Google Tasks, or Microsoft To Do
	•	From content you share into Loom using the Share into Loom extension
3. How Loom Uses Information
	•	To create and manage your account, authenticate sign-in, and keep your session active
	•	To provide Loom’s planning, goal, fulfillment, capture, reminder, attachment, and reflection features
	•	To personalize the app, generate inferred profiles or insights, and tailor setup or AI suggestions to your context
		•	To enable optional integrations you request, such as Health, Reminders, Google Tasks, or Microsoft To Do
	•	To process purchases, restore purchases, and determine premium access through Apple’s StoreKit systems
	•	To improve app quality, reliability, and performance through analytics, diagnostics, and feedback review
	•	To respond to support requests, bug reports, and feedback you submit
4. Where Data Is Stored
On your device
	•	Main app data is stored locally using SwiftData, Application Support files, and UserDefaults
	•	Imported files and photos are stored in local app storage for attachments
	•	Shared content from the Share into Loom extension is first written into Loom’s shared app-group container so the app can ingest it
	•	Google Tasks and Microsoft To Do tokens used by the app are stored locally on your device
Apple sync storage
	•	Loom attempts to use CloudKit-backed SwiftData storage when available
	•	If CloudKit is available, your app content may sync through your private iCloud account for Loom
	•	If CloudKit is unavailable, Loom falls back to local-only storage
5. Third Parties and Service Providers
Loom does not sell your personal information. Loom shares or processes data with the following categories of third parties when needed to operate the app:
	•	Apple for Sign in with Apple, StoreKit purchases, iCloud/CloudKit sync, notifications, HealthKit, EventKit reminders, camera/photos access, and other Apple frameworks you use
	•	Google / Firebase for authentication, analytics, crash diagnostics, Firestore-backed personalization sync for authenticated users, and app feedback submissions
	•	Google if you connect Google Tasks, so the app can read, refresh, and update your Google Tasks data directly with Google’s APIs
	•	Microsoft if you connect Microsoft To Do, so the app can read, refresh, and update your Microsoft To Do data directly with Microsoft Graph
	•	OpenAI and Loom’s backend worker infrastructure for remote LoomAI features that are not handled fully on device
When Loom uses service providers or processors, Loom expects them to protect personal data in a manner consistent with this policy and applicable law.
6. AI Features
	•	Some Loom AI features can run on device using Apple Intelligence when available and enabled on a supported device
	•	Other LoomAI features use Loom’s remote backend and OpenAI, including general chat, purpose profile generation, and auto-write features
	•	Remote AI requests can include the text you send, plus related personalization and planning context needed to answer the request
	•	Based on the code reviewed, the app’s diagnostic Root Cause and Next Direction generation now runs locally on device rather than through the remote AI endpoint
	•	Loom’s backend worker may temporarily cache some AI request or response data and hashed context summaries for performance and reliability
Because some AI features involve off-device processing, do not enter information you do not want processed by Loom’s backend or OpenAI.
7. Optional Permissions and Integrations
		•	Health: You can choose whether to connect Apple Health data. The code reviewed reads selected health metrics and does not write Health data.
		•	Reminders: You can choose whether Loom can read and update Apple Reminders you sync into Capture.
		•	Google Tasks / Microsoft To Do: You can choose whether Loom connects those accounts and stores refresh/access tokens locally so the app can sync your tasks directly with those providers.
		•	Camera and Photos: You can choose whether Loom can use the camera, import photo attachments, or save generated images to Photos.
		•	Notifications: You can choose whether Loom can schedule local reminders and alerts.
You can revoke most app permissions at any time in iOS Settings. You can also disconnect third-party task integrations from within Loom or through the relevant provider account settings.
8. Link Previews, Shared Content, and Attachments
	•	If you add or share a web link, Loom may request link preview metadata to render a preview card
	•	If you share text, links, images, videos, or files into Loom, that content may be written into Loom’s shared app-group storage before you review and save it
	•	Imported files and images are stored locally for attachment features
	•	Based on the code reviewed, Loom does not upload those attachment files to Loom’s servers as standalone files
9. Data Retention
	•	Loom keeps your local app data until you delete it, delete your account, remove the app, or clear the relevant local/app-synced state
	•	If you use CloudKit sync, your synced app data may remain in your private iCloud storage until it is removed through the app or your iCloud account
	•	Firestore personalization records are retained while needed for the signed-in Loom account and are cleared when the app’s account-deletion flow resets that state
	•	Feedback submissions may be retained as support or product records even if you later delete your account
	•	Analytics, crash, and remote AI processing data may be retained for operational, security, fraud-prevention, debugging, legal, or provider-policy reasons
10. Your Choices, Access, and Deletion
	•	You can update some account information inside the app
	•	You can revoke permissions in iOS Settings and disconnect optional integrations you no longer want to use
	•	You can use Loom’s in-app Account > Delete Account flow to delete your account and remove your local app data
	•	For supported sign-in methods, Loom’s deletion flow reauthenticates the account and, for Sign in with Apple, revokes Apple authorization before deleting the Firebase account
	•	Deleting your Loom account does not automatically cancel Apple subscriptions. Apple subscriptions must be canceled separately in Apple Account Settings
	•	If you need privacy help or a deletion-related follow-up, contact spence0927@gmail.com
11. Security
Loom uses reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the app’s size and design. No system can guarantee absolute security.
12. Children’s Privacy
Loom is not intended for children under 13, and Loom does not knowingly build the app to collect personal information from children under 13.
13. International Processing
Loom, its service providers, and its infrastructure providers may process information in countries other than your own.
14. Changes to This Policy
Loom may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version will be posted at this URL with an updated effective date.
